Just noticed this [link](http://msdn.microsoft.com/asp.net/default.aspx?pull=/library/en-us/dnaspp/html/acsaraf1.asp) in [a post](http://weblogs.asp.net/ksharkey/archive/2004/05/27/143141.aspx) on [Kent Sharkeys](http://weblogs.asp.net/ksharkey) blog. The [article](http://msdn.microsoft.com/asp.net/default.aspx?pull=/library/en-us/dnaspp/html/acsaraf1.asp) by [Scott Mitchell](http://scottonwriting.net/)covers various methods of making ASP.NET 1.1 based sites conform to WAI accessibility guidelines (or US Section 508 if you prefer :-)). 
It is still stunningly annoying (and frankly unbelievable) that the [ASP.NET Hotfix Rollup](http://support.microsoft.com/default.aspx?scid=kb;EN-US;821156) is still not easily downloadable - almost a year since it was released, neither have I seen it on the MSDN subscribers download site (although apparently a few CMS 2002 updates actually include it!).
